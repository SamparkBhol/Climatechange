About Climate Change

CONTACT - SEND ME AN EMAIL

Climate change is a significant and lasting change in the statistical distribution of weather patterns over periods ranging from decades to millions of years. It can be a change in the average weather conditions or the distribution of events around that average.

Climate change is primarily caused by human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. These activities release large amounts of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4), into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing the Earth's temperature to rise.

The effects of climate change are far-reaching and include rising sea levels, more frequent and severe heat waves, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events such as hurricanes and droughts. These changes can have devastating impacts on ecosystems, human health, and economies worldwide.
